## Who it's for

Small and mid-size manufacturers across North America — the buyers, ops leads,
and owner-operators who run direct-materials purchasing through three supplier
quotes in three formats, a spreadsheet, and an inbox. No IT project, no supplier
onboarding, no six-week implementation.

## Positioning pillars

1. **The decision is the product.** Everyone digitizes the form-filling; Corven
   makes the messiest step — comparing unlike supplier quotes and choosing —
   fast, explained, and defensible.
2. **Human-approved autonomy.** AI ingests, extracts, matches, and drafts. A
   person approves. Every automated step is a persisted, auditable workflow run.
3. **Day-one value, zero supplier friction.** Suppliers just reply by email —
   they never join anything. The first decision-ready comparison happens in the
   first session, not after onboarding.
4. **Built for quote-based buying.** RFQ → quote ingestion → comparison → PO,
   opinionated defaults over workflow builders. Catalog buying is a different
   sport; Corven doesn't play it.

## Elevator pitch (spoken, ~30 seconds)

"If you buy materials for a manufacturer, your real job is a decision: three
quotes, three formats, one choice you have to defend. The big procurement
suites make that an IT project, and the modern tools mostly digitize the
request form. Corven takes the decision itself — AI reads the quotes however
they arrive, lines them up on real value, drafts the PO, and you approve. Your
suppliers don't change anything; they just reply to an email. You get a
defensible decision in your first session, not after a six-week rollout."

## What Corven never claims

- Not "revolutionary", "magic", or bare "AI-powered" — outcomes over adjectives.
- Never implies the system buys without a human approval.
- Doesn't promise ERP suite breadth, catalog/marketplace buying, or enterprise
  source-to-pay. The wedge is the quote decision; everything else earns its way
  in later.
